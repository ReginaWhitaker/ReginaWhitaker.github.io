How to Outsource Your Life

Step 1: List Tasks to Outsource

The first step

The first step in outsourcing your life is to write down all the domestic and personal tasks you are responsible for daily, weekly, or monthly.

  • Cooking
  • Grocery Shopping
  • Laundry
  • Carwash
  • Bill Pay
  • House Cleaning
  • Yard Work

Step 2: Research Companies to do the Tasks (Outsource)

The next step is to research, via the Internet or personal referral, a company or individual who can do those tasks for you.


If you want to start the system immediately, you can start with these general categories: gas, groceries, allowance, and eating out. These categories encompass a large number of non-mandatory/discretionary expenses.

Step 3: Create an Budget

Start by creating a budget for your monthly bills. This will give you a clear picture of your financial situation. Then, look at your discretionary spending, such as eating out, coffee, and shopping. Consider if you can cut back on these expenses. Once you've identified the least expensive task to outsource, you can start there.


Step 4: Incorporate One Tasks at a Time

Outsourcing can be a significant emotional, mental, and financial change. Many people are used to performing all these tasks independently.

Loosening the hold and allowing someone to take over these tasks can be challenging. For instance, many people delay getting a maid until they clean up a bit because of embarrassment. Remember, they are professionals. They have seen all kinds of homes in different states. This is why you need them.

Many people feel they can't afford to outsource tasks. However, if you examine your spending habits and cut back on frivolous expenses, you may find that you can afford to outsource at least one task. It's a matter of prioritizing your spending.

Don't overwhelm yourself by outsourcing everything at once. Start with one task a month and gradually increase. This approach allows you to learn and adjust to outsourcing over time, leading to a more satisfying experience.


Step 5: Evaluate and Adjust Outsourcing

After fully implementing your outsourcing plan, evaluate whether you are happy with the service or need to find someone else. Not everyone will be an immediate good fit; try a few companies or people to outsource to before you give up.

NOTE:Understand that you are partnering with those you are outsourcing to. Be kind and respectful, and communicate your needs. The best-outsourced experiences are those of cooperation. They have a job to do and seek to have a happy customer experience.


Step 6: Make Plans for Additional Avaialbe Time

Now that you've outsourced, it's time to plan how you'll make the most of your newfound free time. Whether it's pursuing a hobby, spending more time with loved ones, or focusing on self-care, having a plan can help you fully enjoy the benefits of outsourcing.
